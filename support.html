Quick answers

For small things that should not require a whole ticket. If your issue is private/account-specific, use tickets instead of DMs.

Do you answer DMs for support?
No. DMs get flooded and are easy to lose. Please make a ticket so the humans can track it properly.
How long should I wait for ticket review?
Please give support tickets up to 48 hours to be reviewed. If something is urgent, include that clearly in the ticket title and details.
When should I make a ticket?
Make a ticket for billing, refunds, account access, broken containers, private information, channel connection problems, or anything you would not want in public Discord.
When should I ask Discord?
Use Discord for general learning, ideas, prompts, examples, community questions, and “how would you build this?” conversations.
My agent is not responding. What should I try first?
Refresh the dashboard, check the connected channel, and try one clean test message. If it still fails, make a ticket with screenshots and what you tried.
Where do I set up my agent?
Start in Launchpad. It walks you through your name, agent name, first mission, tools, and guardrails.
How do refunds work?
You can request a refund in Billing Center. Refunds are reviewed by humans and are not guaranteed after three days of active use from purchase time.
Can I change my email or card?
Yes. Use Billing Center so the request becomes a supervised ticket with the right details.
What should I include in a ticket?
Your account email, agent/container name if you know it, screenshots, what you expected, what happened, and the exact channel/tool involved.
Who is watching support?
The Heyron Command Center: Commander Robby and Captain Cassie help with human review, and Ron keeps the board organized.
